Timeline of Reining History

Major milestones, pivotal moments, and the evolution of the sport across six decades

1949

NRHA Founded

The National Reining Horse Association is established, creating the first organized governing body for the sport of reining and standardizing rules of competition across the country.

1966

NRHA Futurity Inaugural Year

The NRHA Futurity — the most prestigious reining event in the world — holds its inaugural competition in Oklahoma City, creating the premier proving ground for 3-year-old reining horses.

1983

Hollywood Dun It Foaled

Hollywood Dun It arrives, destined to become one of the most influential reining sires of all time. His get would go on to win millions in NRHA competition worldwide across multiple decades.

1990

Taris Little Step Wins Open Futurity

Taris Little Step captures the NRHA Open Futurity, one of the most celebrated victories of the era and a defining moment for breeding and training standards in reining.

2000

Reining Recognized by the FEI

Reining is officially recognized by the Fédération Equestre Internationale (FEI), opening the door for international competition and growth of the sport across Europe and beyond.

2002

First FEI World Reining Championship

The inaugural FEI World Reining Championships is held, marking a new global era for the sport and showcasing American reining legends on the international stage for the first time.

2010

Reining at the World Equestrian Games

Reining is featured at the World Equestrian Games in Lexington, Kentucky — the pinnacle of international horse sport. American riders dominate competition on the world stage.

2020s

Era of Record Earners

A new generation of breeders and trainers push earnings and performance to unprecedented heights. NRHA Futurity purses reach all-time records as the sport expands into new markets globally.

About This Archive

Reining Hall of Fame is an independent editorial and historical archive celebrating the legends, horses, riders, trainers, and history of the sport of reining. This is not an official NRHA website.

Our mission is to preserve and celebrate the deep heritage of reining — honoring the people, horses, and moments that built one of the world’s most skilled western equestrian disciplines into the global sport it is today.
